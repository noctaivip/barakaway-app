What it means to perform Qurban properly

Qurban is a sacrifice offered for Allah during the days of Eid al-Adha. Performing it properly means more than completing an outward ritual; it means having the right intention, respecting the worship and caring for people.

The meaning of Qurban is connected with obedience, gratitude and mercy. A Muslim should understand why it is done, how the meat is shared and what mistakes should be avoided.

Intention before Qurban

Every act of worship begins with intention. Qurban is not done for praise, status, habit or social pressure, but for Allah’s pleasure and nearness to Him.

The intention does not have to be spoken aloud. What matters is that the heart knows the sacrifice is for Allah.

Who performs Qurban

Details of the ruling may differ by school and personal situation, so a Muslim should ask a reliable local scholar for specific cases.

In general, Qurban is connected with ability. A person who has the means should not treat this worship lightly.

«Qurbonlikning qadri tashqi ko‘rinishda emas, balki taqvo, ixlosli niyat va bo‘lishishga tayyorlikdadir».

The animal and its condition

The animal should be of a valid type, healthy and free from serious defects that make it unsuitable for sacrifice.

Islamic etiquette also requires kindness to the animal, cleanliness and avoiding unnecessary fear or suffering.

Time of Qurban

Qurban is performed during the prescribed days of Eid al-Adha. It should not be reduced to an ordinary purchase of meat without respect for time and worship.

Good preparation prevents haste, disorder and disrespect toward the ritual.

How to distribute the meat

The meat may benefit the family, relatives, neighbours and the needy. A common practice is to keep a portion, gift a portion and give a portion to the poor.

The main point is generosity and care, especially for poor families, orphans, neighbours and relatives in difficulty.

Common mistakes

A common mistake is seeing Qurban only as meat, losing the spiritual meaning of obedience, gratitude and mercy.

Other mistakes include showing off, arguing, neglecting cleanliness, ignoring people’s rights and treating animals badly.

Conclusion

Proper Qurban joins knowledge, intention, taqwa and mercy. It respects the conditions of sacrifice, the time of Eid, the animal and the people who receive the meat.

Such Qurban helps the believer feel the meaning of Eid al-Adha: closeness to Allah, gratitude and willingness to share.
