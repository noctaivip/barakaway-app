Why This Project Is Anonymous

Not as a feature — but as a principle

BarakaWay is anonymous by design.

Not because something is hidden — but because the system must remain clean.

Anonymity is not a limitation. It is a protection layer.

Protecting intention

In charity, intention defines everything.

When a project becomes attached to specific individuals, attention slowly shifts from helping people to focusing on who is behind the project.

Anonymity removes that shift and keeps the focus exactly where it belongs: on the act of helping — not the people behind it.

Protection from pressure and distortion

Public identity always creates influence.

Requests start coming in: promote this fundraiser, support this family, make an exception, prioritize this case.

Even when such requests come with good intentions, they gradually distort the decision-making process.

Anonymity removes this layer of pressure and protects the internal independence of the project.

Fair selection of families

This project is built as a system, not as a reaction to personal requests.

Each family should be considered based on the reality of their situation, not on personal connections, pressure, emotion, or outside influence.

Anonymity helps decisions remain calm, clear, and fair.

Protecting dignity

Not every family wants its hardship exposed.

Not every difficulty should be turned into content.

We do not build this project on people’s pain.

Dignity comes before visibility.

Focus only on what matters

This project is not about individuals.

It is not about creators.

It is not about recognition.

It is about one thing: helping people correctly.

Sometimes the strongest charity is the one that remains unseen.

“If you disclose your charity, it is good. But if you conceal it and give it to the poor, it is better for you.”

Qur’an 2:271
