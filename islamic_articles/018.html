Justice and Mercy in Islam

How balance between fairness and compassion shapes a Muslim’s responsibility

Islam is built on justice and mercy. These principles guide how Muslims interact with others and create a fair society.

Justice ensures rights are protected, while mercy softens hearts and brings compassion.

Justice as a Foundation

Justice means giving every person what they deserve. It applies to all people equally.

Muslims are required to act fairly in all aspects of life.

Mercy as a Way of Life

Mercy is central in Islam. Believers are encouraged to be kind, forgiving, and supportive.

Helping others through sadaqa is one of the clearest forms of mercy.

Balance Between Justice and Mercy

A balanced life requires both justice and mercy. One without the other creates imbalance.

Islam teaches combining fairness with compassion.

The Role of Sadaqa

Sadaqa supports those in need and strengthens society.

Even a small act of charity can bring meaningful change.

“Indeed, Allah commands justice and excellence...”

Qur’an 16:90
Justice with mercy creates a stronger and more compassionate society.
